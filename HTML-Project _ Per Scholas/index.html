Chocolate Fudge

Description

Chocolate fudge is a rich and decadent confectionery treat made from a combination of sugar, butter, milk, and chocolate. It has a smooth and creamy texture with a deep chocolate flavor that melts in your mouth. Chocolate fudge is often enjoyed as a dessert or snack and is a popular choice for gift-giving during holidays and special occasions.

The origins of chocolate fudge can be traced back to the late 19th century in the United States. It is believed that the first batch of fudge was accidentally created when a batch of caramel candy did not set properly, resulting in a soft and creamy texture. This mistake led to the creation of what we now know as fudge.

Fudge quickly gained popularity and became a staple in candy shops and homes across the country. Its rich and indulgent flavor made it a favorite treat for people of all ages. Over the years, different variations of fudge, including chocolate fudge, were developed to cater to different tastes and preferences.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter (optional for creaminess)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional for flavor)
  • Pinch of salt (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Pan: Line an 8x8-inch square baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang for easy removal later.
  2. Melt the Ingredients: In a large saucepan over low heat, combine the chocolate chips, sweetened condensed milk, and butter (if using). Stir constantly until the mixture is smooth and fully melted. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Be careful not to let it burn..
  3. Add Flavor: Once melted, remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt, if desired. This enhances the flavor of the fudge..
  4. Pour and Set: Pour the fudge mixture into the prepared pan, spreading it evenly with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to remove any air bubbles..
  5. Chill: Refrigerate the fudge for at least 2 hours or until firm. If you're in a hurry, you can place it in the freezer for about 1 hour..
  6. Cut and Serve: Once set, lift the fudge out of the pan using the parchment paper overhang. Cut into small squares and enjoy!
